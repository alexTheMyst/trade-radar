## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Hybrid

**Description:**
This phase operationalizes an existing AI-assisted investment-alert pipeline into runnable batch jobs. The core AI behavior is thesis-driven news classification using the Anthropic SDK with structured outputs; the rest of the phase wires that classifier into heartbeat-monitored jobs, deterministic routing, SQLite persistence, and digest delivery. Good behavior means the jobs produce reliable, auditable, budget-capped signals without silently dropping parse failures, skipping zero-alert confirmations, or bypassing the existing safety/review boundaries.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. News headlines that should produce a thesis-relevant signal are silently dropped or misclassified without a MONITORING fallback.
2. Routed outputs are persisted or emailed incorrectly, causing the operator to miss a material alert or receive a misleading digest.
3. Batch-job orchestration breaks the classifier's existing structured-output, sanitization, or audit-trail guarantees.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** finance

**User Population:** solo investment operator monitoring held positions across multiple Schwab accounts

**Stakes Level:** High

**Output Consequence:** AI output influences which thesis-relevant developments get surfaced for manual trading decisions; bad outputs can cause missed material events, noisy false positives, or misplaced operator attention.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

**Dimension:** Thesis-event relevance  
**Good (domain expert would accept):** The alert names the correct held ticker, ties the news to a specific thesis pillar, and explains the concrete event in investing terms (for example: guidance cut, regulatory setback, major customer win, capital allocation change, management turnover).  
**Bad (domain expert would flag):** Generic sector commentary, macro chatter, or a peer-company story is surfaced as if it changed the held company's thesis; issuer/ticker mapping is wrong or vague.  
**Stakes:** Critical  
**Source:** Project core value ("never miss a material thesis-relevant event") + arXiv:2407.15788 on financial-news ticker extraction + arXiv:2404.01338 on detecting relevant financial events

**Dimension:** Materiality over inbox noise  
**Good (domain expert would accept):** The digest surfaces developments that can plausibly change earnings power, balance-sheet risk, regulatory exposure, capital allocation, or management credibility; routine mentions and recycled commentary stay out of the delivered section.  
**Bad (domain expert would flag):** The system spends alert budget on low-signal items such as minor blog mentions, generic analyst chatter, routine price-move writeups, or duplicate coverage of the same event.  
**Stakes:** High  
**Source:** Event-driven investing practice + arXiv:2105.12825 ("corporate events as the driving force behind stock movements")

**Dimension:** Timeliness around the decision window  
**Good (domain expert would accept):** The morning job captures overnight and pre-open items since the prior close, and the digest reaches the operator while the news is still decision-relevant.  
**Bad (domain expert would flag):** A material overnight filing is missing until after the open, or an already-processed/stale headline is re-presented as fresh.  
**Stakes:** Critical  
**Source:** Phase 06 requirements/goal + arXiv:2404.01338 on relevance and temporality + arXiv:2105.12825 on minute-level timestamps for event-driven trading

**Dimension:** Source trust and rumor handling  
**Good (domain expert would accept):** Confirmed sources such as company filings, earnings releases, regulator actions, or top-tier wires are treated as stronger evidence; rumors, promotional content, and unattributed social posts are suppressed or downgraded to monitoring.  
**Bad (domain expert would flag):** Anonymous social-media chatter or stock-promotion content is surfaced with the same weight as a filing or company announcement.  
**Stakes:** High  
**Source:** Investor.gov, "Internet and Social Media Fraud" — online investment content can be made to look real and credible and should not be treated at face value

**Dimension:** Confidence honesty  
**Good (domain expert would accept):** Confidence is reserved for clear, attributable, thesis-linked events; ambiguous headlines, mixed evidence, or uncertain entity resolution are shown with caution or routed to monitoring instead of sounding decisive.  
**Bad (domain expert would flag):** The system sounds highly certain on thin evidence, uses precise-sounding confidence for borderline cases, or hides uncertainty that should slow a trading decision.  
**Stakes:** High  
**Source:** Operator constraint to avoid misleading confidence + arXiv:2311.08298 on LLM confidence estimation/calibration

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

- Wrong issuer resolution: the system attaches a headline to the wrong ticker, parent/subsidiary, peer, or same-name company and creates a false thesis hit.
- Stale or duplicated event handling: the same story is resurfaced as new, or an overnight event is delivered too late to be useful at the morning decision point.
- Rumor escalation: unattributed social chatter, promotional newsletters, or other low-credibility items are treated like confirmed company news.
- Silent no-alert ambiguity: a missing digest on a quiet day is indistinguishable from a job failure, so the operator cannot tell "nothing important happened" from "the monitoring system broke."

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

- No adviser-specific or automated-execution regime is directly triggered by this phase's stated deployment: it is a private, alert-only workflow and project rules explicitly forbid automated trade execution.
- Source discipline still matters. Investor.gov warns that internet/social-media investment content can appear credible while being fraudulent or manipulative, so rumor-like content should not be treated as sufficient evidence for a delivered alert.
- If the operator is employed by a regulated firm or is a FINRA-associated person, personal trading rules may apply outside the software itself. FINRA Rule 3210 can require written consent/account disclosure and duplicate confirms/statements for outside brokerage accounts.

### Domain Expert Roles for Evaluation

| Role | Responsibility in Eval |
|------|------------------------|
| Investment operator / thesis owner | Label whether an event was genuinely thesis-relevant, calibrate materiality thresholds, and review false negatives from production samples |

| Trading-ops owner (same solo operator in this deployment) | Review zero-alert days, digest usefulness, timing around market open, and whether the inbox noise level is actually tradable |
| Compliance reviewer (only if operator is subject to employer personal-account rules) | Confirm the workflow, logs, and account usage do not conflict with employer/FINRA personal trading obligations |

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Direct Anthropic SDK + existing in-repo orchestration

**Version:** `anthropic==0.102.0`

**Rationale:**
Phase 06 is not a greenfield agent framework build. The repository already has a working Anthropic-based classifier, direct SQLite persistence, explicit heartbeat wrapping, and a pure alert router. Adding LangChain, CrewAI, or another orchestration framework would introduce abstraction overhead and conflict with the repo's locked "no frameworks" guidance for this workflow. The best fit is to keep the direct-SDK architecture, treat this as a hybrid batch-orchestration system around an existing AI component, and extend the current codebase with thin orchestration only.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| LangGraph | Strong for durable stateful workflows, but overkill for this thin batch-job orchestration phase and adds new framework complexity. |
| LangChain | Broad ecosystem, but introduces abstraction overhead and conflicts with the repo's preference for direct SDK usage in this system. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Partial — the classifier is already Anthropic-specific, but the orchestration layer remains plain Python and repository-owned.

### Entry Point Pattern
```python
from signal_system import config
from signal_system.classifier.news_classifier import classify_headlines
from signal_system.data.thesis_loader import load_thesis
from signal_system.monitoring import heartbeat
from signal_system.router.alert_router import route_signals
from signal_system.state import repository


def run_news_batch(headlines_by_ticker: dict[str, list[dict]]) -> None:
    run_id = repository.insert_run("news-morning")
    try:
        with heartbeat.heartbeat():
            thesis, thesis_version_hash = load_thesis(config.THESIS_PATH)
            dedup_seen: set[str] = set()
            routable = []

            for ticker, headlines in headlines_by_ticker.items():
                for signal in classify_headlines(
                    ticker=ticker,
                    headlines=headlines,
                    thesis=thesis,
                    thesis_version_hash=thesis_version_hash,
                    dedup_seen=dedup_seen,
                ):
                    if signal.severity == "MONITORING":
                        repository.insert_signal(signal, routing_status="MONITORING")
                    else:
                        routable.append(signal)

            for signal, routing_status, demoted_from in route_signals(routable):
                repository.insert_signal(
                    signal,
                    routing_status=routing_status,
                    demoted_from=demoted_from,
                )

            repository.update_run(run_id, "success")
    except Exception:
        repository.update_run(run_id, "failed")
        raise
```

### Key Abstractions
| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `Anthropic()` client singleton | Long-lived SDK client reused across calls (`_get_client()`) | Initialize once per process; do not recreate per headline |
| `client.messages.parse(..., output_format=...)` | Claude response parsed directly into a Pydantic schema | Use for headline classification where schema compliance matters more than free-form prose |
| `ClassificationResult` Pydantic model | Contract for `pillar_name`, `confidence`, `direction`, `rationale` | Use as the only model-facing schema for the classifier output |
| `heartbeat.heartbeat()` run boundary | Required healthcheck wrapper around all scheduled jobs | Wrap the full job body so network/DB failures trip `/fail` |
| `route_signals()` + `repository.insert_signal()` | Pure routing followed by caller-owned persistence | Use after classification for non-`MONITORING` signals; bypass router for `MONITORING` |

### Common Pitfalls
1. **Creating a new Anthropic client per headline** — adds connection overhead and makes batch latency/cost noisier; keep the existing module-level singleton.
2. **Treating `messages.parse()` validation failures as hard drops** — Pydantic/schema mismatches happen; if you do not retry and emit a `MONITORING` fallback, the operator loses auditability.
3. **Sending `MONITORING` signals into `route_signals()`** — the router explicitly rejects them; persist them directly with `routing_status="MONITORING"`.
4. **Moving `repository.update_run(run_id, "success")` outside the heartbeat block** — hides DB write failures from Healthchecks and breaks the repo's locked job contract.
5. **Rebuilding or reordering the thesis prompt per headline** — Anthropic prompt caching is block-based; unstable system prompt text reduces cache hits and raises spend.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**
- Keep the repo-configured model in `config.ANTHROPIC_MODEL`; for Phase 06, standardize on `claude-sonnet-4-6` unless the operator overrides it in `.env`.
- Classification call shape should stay:
  - `temperature=0.0`
  - `max_tokens=512`
  - `output_format=ClassificationResult`
  - `system=[{"type": "text", "text": system_prompt, "cache_control": {"type": "ephemeral"}}]`
- Do **not** enable extended thinking for this classifier. It increases latency/token spend and adds no value to a four-field extraction task.

**Core Pattern:**
Use the Anthropic SDK only for the narrow AI step; keep fetching, routing, persistence, and digesting deterministic in Python:

```python
from collections import Counter

from signal_system import config
from signal_system.classifier.news_classifier import classify_headlines
from signal_system.data.thesis_loader import load_thesis
from signal_system.delivery import email_sender
from signal_system.monitoring import heartbeat
from signal_system.router.alert_router import route_signals
from signal_system.state import repository


def run_news_morning(headlines_by_ticker: dict[str, list[dict]]) -> None:
    run_id = repository.insert_run("news-morning")
    try:
        with heartbeat.heartbeat():
            thesis, thesis_version_hash = load_thesis(config.THESIS_PATH)  # fail fast on stale/missing thesis
            dedup_seen: set[str] = set()
            routable = []
            counts = Counter()

            for ticker, headlines in headlines_by_ticker.items():
                # Headline cap/dedup should happen before this function is called.
                for signal in classify_headlines(
                    ticker=ticker,
                    headlines=headlines,
                    thesis=thesis,
                    thesis_version_hash=thesis_version_hash,
                    dedup_seen=dedup_seen,
                ):
                    if signal.severity == "MONITORING":
                        repository.insert_signal(signal, routing_status="MONITORING")
                        counts["MONITORING"] += 1
                    else:
                        routable.append(signal)

            routed = route_signals(routable)  # pure function; DB writes stay below
            delivered = []
            for signal, routing_status, demoted_from in routed:
                repository.insert_signal(signal, routing_status=routing_status, demoted_from=demoted_from)
                counts[routing_status] += 1
                if routing_status == "DELIVERED":
                    delivered.append(signal)

            # One digest per run, including explicit zero-alert confirmation.
            body = [
                f"Delivered: {len(delivered)}",
                f"Suppressed: {counts['SUPPRESSED']}",
                f"Monitoring: {counts['MONITORING']}",
            ]
            if not delivered:
                body.append("Scanned headlines, 0 alerts delivered.")

            email_sender.send_email(
                subject="News Morning Digest",
                body="\n".join(body),
            )
            repository.update_run(run_id, "success")  # keep inside heartbeat
    except Exception:
        repository.update_run(run_id, "failed")
        raise
```

Discovery should reuse the same persistence/digest path, but call `score_universe()` instead of Claude. In Discovery Phase A, do not send email; in Phase B, route returned signals and send the same zero-alert digest behavior.

**Tool Use:**
- **Anthropic tool use:** disabled for this phase. Do not pass `tools=` or `tool_choice=` to Claude; Finnhub, SQLite, SMTP, and router access stay in trusted Python code.
- **Structured output integration:** keep `client.messages.parse(..., output_format=ClassificationResult)` for the classifier.
- **Retry integration:** keep `tenacity` around `ValidationError` only; do not retry logic errors, stale thesis errors, or router failures.
- **Tracing/telemetry:** keep using `repository.insert_llm_call(...)`, `runs`, `signals`, and standard logging. Phase 06 does not need Langfuse/LangSmith just to wire batch jobs.

**State Management:**
- SQLite remains the system of record.
- Every public job follows: `insert_run()` → `with heartbeat.heartbeat():` → work → `update_run(..., "success")` inside the context → `update_run(..., "failed")` in `except`.
- Persist all signals through `repository.insert_signal(...)`; jobs must not write raw SQL.
- `alert_id` and `INSERT OR IGNORE` give idempotent signal writes, but email sending is not idempotent; document rerun behavior and avoid automatic reruns that resend digests.
- Use the `runs` table as orchestration state too: Phase 06 should add/read a helper for the latest successful `daily-close` so `news-morning` uses a market-close anchor instead of naïve calendar math.

**Context Window Strategy:**
- Keep the classifier at **one headline per Claude call**. This is the right trade-off for reliability, auditability, and failure isolation.
- Preserve the existing `_sanitize_headline()` truncation to 500 chars before prompt insertion.
- Deduplicate before calling Claude, then apply the 50-headline cap newest-first; overflow becomes deterministic `MONITORING` rows rather than more model context.
- Reuse one stable system prompt per ticker batch so Anthropic prompt caching can reduce repeated input cost.
- Discovery and digest generation should stay non-LLM. Do not spend context window on summarizing results that can be rendered deterministically.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice.

### Structured Outputs with Pydantic

```python
from typing import Literal

from pydantic import BaseModel, Field


class HeadlineClassification(BaseModel):
    pillar_name: str | None = Field(
        description="Matched thesis pillar name, or null if the headline is off-thesis."
    )
    confidence: float = Field(ge=0.0, le=1.0)
    direction: Literal["positive", "negative", "neutral"]
    rationale: str = Field(min_length=1, max_length=280)
```

Use it exactly with the Anthropic SDK's structured-output path:

```python
parsed = client.messages.parse(
    model=config.ANTHROPIC_MODEL,
    max_tokens=512,
    temperature=0.0,
    system=[{"type": "text", "text": system_prompt, "cache_control": {"type": "ephemeral"}}],
    messages=[{"role": "user", "content": sanitized_headline}],
    output_format=HeadlineClassification,
)
result = parsed.parsed_output
```

- **Retry policy:** keep the current `tenacity` pattern: retry `ValidationError` only, stop after **2 total attempts**, wait **1 second**.
- **Log on failure:** ticker, alert ID, validation error text, model version, and any recovery-call raw response truncated to the same safety limit already used in `news_classifier.py`.
- **When to surface:** after the second failed parse, persist a `MONITORING` signal instead of dropping the headline. Only let the job fail hard for transport/auth/config problems, not for one malformed model response.

### Async-First Design

The Anthropic SDK supports `AsyncAnthropic`, but this repo is intentionally synchronous today. Keep Phase 06 sync unless you are converting the whole job boundary.

- **How async works here:** `AsyncAnthropic().messages.create/parse(...)` is available if future work parallelizes Finnhub fetches or headline classification.
- **Common mistake:** calling `asyncio.run()` from an existing event loop (tests, notebooks, or future service wrappers). If the repo ever goes async, expose one top-level `async def run_*()` and `await` it from the real entrypoint.
- **Stream vs. await:** use full awaited responses for structured classification because Pydantic validation needs the completed payload. Streaming is only worth it for human-facing interactive tooling, not scheduled batch jobs.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

- Keep **thesis and behavioral rules in the system prompt**; keep the sanitized headline as the only user message.
- Do not concatenate router state, DB metadata, or email-formatting instructions into the classifier prompt.
- Few-shot examples should be added only if misclassification data proves they are needed. Prefer a short curated set stored in code or config, not ad hoc examples inline in each job.
- Set `max_tokens=512` explicitly on every classifier call. Do not leave Claude output unbounded in production batch jobs.

### Context Window Management

- `news-morning` is **not** a long-context agent. The correct pattern is: fetch bounded news window → dedup → cap at 50 → classify one headline at a time.
- Use the last successful `daily-close` run as the lower window bound so the job does not inflate context and API volume on Mondays/holidays.
- Do not ask Claude to summarize the entire batch before routing; that makes failures less auditable and mixes multiple decisions into one context.
- If a future digest-summary LLM step is added, summarize only delivered alerts and keep MONITORING/SUPPRESSED results count-only.

### Cost and Latency Budget

- Discovery costs **0 LLM calls** in Phase 06; only `news-morning` spends tokens.
- Budget for **at most 50 Claude calls per `news-morning` run** after dedup/cap. With short headlines, `temperature=0`, and a cached system prompt, the expected spend is **low single-digit cents per normal run and should remain under roughly $0.10 even on a full-cap day**; verify against Anthropic's current pricing page before go-live.
- Track actual spend via `llm_calls` rows, not guesswork. Alert on unexpected call-count growth before switching models.
- Use two caching layers:
  1. **Exact-match semantic equivalent:** the existing headline dedup key prevents duplicate classification calls.
  2. **Provider-side prompt caching:** keep the thesis system prompt stable and marked cacheable.
- Do not add a second cheaper model in Phase 06. First exhaust deterministic optimizations (windowing, dedup, cap, direct routing) before introducing model-routing complexity.

## 6. Guardrails

### Online (Real-Time)

| Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| Fail closed on invalid run boundary | Stale thesis, missing thesis, or no valid previous successful `daily-close` anchor for `news-morning` | **Block** the run, mark failed, send `/fail`; do not guess the news window |
| Preserve MONITORING audit trail | Classifier parse failure after retry, or headline skipped by 50-headline cap | **Auto-flag** by persisting a `MONITORING` row with the reason; exclude from delivered alerts |
| Enforce digest completeness | Live `news-morning` or Discovery Phase B run ends without exactly one digest, or digest counts do not match persisted statuses, or zero delivered alerts lack explicit confirmation text | **Block success / escalate** by failing the run instead of allowing silent completion |

### Offline (Flywheel)

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| Thesis relevance false positives / false negatives | Review all delivered alerts for first 2 weeks, then 20% sample weekly; add extra review for zero-alert days | Refine rubric/prompt wording, add counterexamples, expand fixture set |
| Materiality / inbox-noise rate | Sample all suppressed alerts on budget-saturated days and 10 random non-delivered outcomes per week | Tighten routing thresholds or classifier instructions if low-value items dominate |
| Confidence honesty / rumor handling | Review all low-credibility-source items and any high-confidence delivered alert with ambiguous sourcing | Add downgrade heuristics or explicit source-handling examples; increase human spot checks |
| Timeliness drift | Review every run with late send time, cap overflow, or repeated stale/duplicate handling | Adjust schedule/window logic, inspect dedup logic, and add regression fixtures |
| Parse-failure and MONITORING trend | Batch review every parse-failure `MONITORING` item weekly | Fix schema/prompt drift before it becomes a silent-quality issue |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** Arize Phoenix

**Key Metrics to Track:**
- Job success rate by job name (`news-morning`, `discovery`, deferred backfill when activated)
- Digest coverage rate: successful live runs with exactly one email sent
- Delivered / suppressed / monitoring counts per run
- Parse-failure `MONITORING` count and 50-headline overflow count
- `news-morning` LLM calls, tokens, and estimated cost per run
- End-to-end run duration and digest send time relative to scheduled morning window

**Alert Thresholds:**
- **Page immediately:** no `news-morning` success by the expected morning SLA on scheduled days, or any live run finishes without its required digest
- **Page immediately:** any run exceeds the hard cap of **50** classifier calls for `news-morning`
- **Investigate same day:** parse-failure `MONITORING` rate > **10%** in a single run or > **5%** over the rolling last 7 runs
- **Investigate same day:** headline-overflow count > **0** for 3 consecutive `news-morning` runs
- **Investigate weekly:** five consecutive zero-delivered `news-morning` runs with non-zero headlines scanned

**Smart Sampling Strategy:**
- Review **100%** of delivered alerts for the first 2 weeks after go-live.
- Always sample runs with: zero delivered alerts, any parse-failure `MONITORING`, any cap overflow, any low-credibility source, or unusual token/cost spikes.
- After baseline stabilizes, sample **20%** of delivered alerts, **20%** of suppressed alerts on budget-hit days, and **all** parse-failure / rumor-like cases.
- When metrics and operator experience diverge ("looks noisy" or "I think we missed something"), treat that as a monitoring event and add a new eval fixture.
